Our lab develops practical AI technologies that can learn from limited, imperfect, and real-world data. Our research focuses on computer vision, machine learning, and multimodal AI, with applications in medical AI, surveillance, scientific discovery, and trustworthy AI systems.

<p align="center">
  <img src="/assets/img/research_overview.png" alt="Research Overview" width="100%">
</p>

<div class="research-grid">

<div class="research-card">
<h3>Weakly/Semi-supervised Learning</h3>
<p>We study methods that reduce annotation costs by learning from weak, noisy, or partially labeled data, especially for visual recognition and segmentation.</p>
</div>

<div class="research-card">
<h3>Active Learning and Data-efficient AI</h3>
<p>We develop active learning methods that select informative samples under limited labeling budgets, with a focus on low-budget and real-world annotation scenarios.</p>
</div>

<div class="research-card">
<h3>Domain Adaptation and Generalization</h3>
<p>We aim to build robust models that maintain performance under distribution shifts across domains, environments, sensors, and institutions.</p>
</div>

<div class="research-card">
<h3>Multimodal AI</h3>
<p>We explore AI systems that combine vision, language, audio, and other modalities for richer real-world understanding.</p>
</div>

<div class="research-card">
<h3>AI + X</h3>
<p>We apply AI to interdisciplinary domains such as healthcare, science, engineering, and real-world systems, with an emphasis on reliability, interpretability, and practical impact.</p>
</div>

</div>
